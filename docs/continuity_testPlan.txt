ARTEMIS HARNESS TESTER - CONTINUITY TEST PLAN
==========================================

Objective
---------
Verify that each channel (CH0–CH20) is correctly wired:
- From Artemis pin
- Through series resistor
- To the correct J1 terminal
- With no shorts between channels

Equipment
---------
- Digital multimeter with continuity / resistance mode
- Access to J1 terminal block
- Access to Artemis pins or test pads for CH0–CH20

Step 1 – Visual Inspection
--------------------------
[ ] Confirm all CAT5 terminations are fully seated and strain-relieved.
[ ] Confirm J1 labeling matches CH0–CH20 mapping.
[ ] Confirm Artemis pins are wired to the correct series resistors (RDRV0–RDRV20).

Step 2 – Per-Channel Continuity (Low-Ohms Check)
------------------------------------------------
For each channel N (0–20):

1. Place one probe on the Artemis pin for CHN.
2. Place the other probe on the corresponding J1 terminal.
3. Expected reading:
   - Approximately 1,000 ohms (series resistor RDRVN).
4. Record result as PASS if resistance is within tolerance (see BOM).

Example:
- CH0: Artemis PIN2 ↔ J1-1 ≈ 1,000 ohms → PASS
- CH1: Artemis PIN3 ↔ J1-2 ≈ 1,000 ohms → PASS
...
- CH20: Artemis PIN22 ↔ J1-21 ≈ 1,000 ohms → PASS

Step 3 – Short-Circuit Check Between Channels
---------------------------------------------
1. Leave one probe on J1-1 (CH0).
2. Sweep the other probe across J1-2 through J1-21.
3. Expected reading:
   - Open circuit (no continuity) between CH0 and all other channels.
4. Repeat for:
   - J1-2 vs J1-3..J1-21
   - J1-3 vs J1-4..J1-21
   - Continue until all pairs are checked.

Any low resistance between channels indicates a wiring short → FAIL.

Step 4 – Ground Isolation Check
-------------------------------
1. Place one probe on tester PCB ground.
2. Sweep the other probe across J1-1 through J1-21.
3. Expected reading:
   - Open circuit (no direct short to ground) on all channels.
   - Exception: if a specific channel is intentionally tied to ground in the harness under test (not in the bare harness cable).

Step 5 – Functional Sanity Check (Optional)
-------------------------------------------
Once firmware is loaded:
1. Connect the harness to J1.
2. Run the SELFTEST or MEASURE command from the PC CLI.
3. Confirm:
   - No unexpected shorts reported.
   - Open channels behave as expected.
   - Known test harnesses produce repeatable results.

Notes
-----
- Refer to the Bill of Materials (BOM) for exact resistor values and tolerances.
- If any channel fails continuity or isolation, rework the harness before use.
